📜 What is Simple Past Tense?

Simple Past Tense is used to describe actions or events that happened and were completed in the past. In Aesop's Fables, the entire story is told using this tense.

SVOC Pattern Subject + Verb₂ (past) + Object + Complement

Each part of the pattern plays a specific role:

Part Role Example
S — Subject Who did the action The fox
V — Verb₂ The action (past form) called
O — Object Who/what received the action the crow
C — Complement Describes the object's result/state foolish
✦ Full Example from Aesop:
"The lion made the mouse his servant."
S: The lion  |  V₂: made  |  O: the mouse  |  C: his servant
